Restaurant Promo

Problem

The company needed to create a new mobile app product for clients in the restaurant business. The concept was a lottery system that allowed customers to collect restaurant’s promotion products with a way to display these coupons for later use.


Project Goals
  • Finish the design within 2 weeks
  • An easy to use interface that is interchangeable for different restaurant clients
  • Contains all the features that the PM was looking for such as: an interactive way to generate a new coupon, coupon storage, and application of coupons

Approach

Normally, I’d like to conduct some user research or interview but because of the limited time frame and my role purely as a design, I was only able to gather input from employees of the company. I also started looking at other coupon related apps to see what the flow would be like to draw inspiration.


My focus on how a customer would interact with it and had some employees use the prototype to see if the flow made sense. I used Figma to design what the app would look like and used Figma’s prototype tool to do my demonstrations.


Feedback and Improvements

I had to juggle between what made sense to me and what made sense to other users. With the help of the QA employee in the company, I made various changes based on how different mobile devices would access the same page. Some changes I made:

  • Tweaked the border sizes so they are not overwhelming - especially on a smaller device
  • Added error pages just in case something were to go wrong with the backend

E-Gift Card

Problem

The company needed to create an online gift card platform for their client - Holt Renfrew in time for Chinese New Year. This project was primarily a re-design and my focus was on improving the existing app to eliminate bugs and other user errors that were discovered from last year.


Project Goals
  • 1 month deadline for design and creation to present to client
  • Fix all of last year’s bugs
  • Improve customer traffic
  • Overcome language barriers - I am not fluent in Chinese but the app was primarily in Chinese

There were quite a few bugs I had to tackle and due to being behind schedule. I ended up taking on the front-end coding for our team.


Approach
  • Fixed the bugs from last year - made necessary changes to buttons and fonts so that the app was compatible on all devices whereas information would cut off on smaller screens before
  • Added error pages which did not exist previously
  • Changed the user flow so customers understand the process better

My idea was a simple design because I knew the company wanted to expand and offer this product to other clients. So my idea was to keep it simple so that when other clients are added, they can incorporate their own design and logos.


Result

After running some QA tests, we set the product to launch and sales doubled from last year’s.
